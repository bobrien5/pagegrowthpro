1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners useful information.

Cookies can be "persistent" (they remain on your device until deleted or they expire) or "session" cookies (they are deleted when you close your browser). They can be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third parties ("third-party cookies").

In addition to our own cookies, the following third parties may set cookies on your device when you use our platform:

4. How to Manage Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to manage cookies through their settings. You can typically find these in the "Options," "Preferences," or "Settings" menu of your browser. The following links provide instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari

Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note that disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in and using the PageGrowthPro platform. Disabling functional cookies may degrade your experience, and disabling analytics cookies will not affect platform functionality.
